Hardware Projects:


HEGduino V1 and 2 DIY Hemoencephalography, made to bring this obscure form of biofeedback into the maker world. Inspired by Hershel Toomim and Bob Marsh's original patent 5995857. I crowdfunded both versions modestly and have sold somewhere over 500 units total between both versions. It's my first real hardware project that I led and built mostly out of my cabin in Alaska with a little help from my friends.
HEG Alpha Site
nRF52 Biosensing Boards Ongoing project to utilise Fanstel nRF52 chips and Zephyr RTOS. Our first prototypes work and with Jacob Tinkhauser and Bojan Jovanovich we developed several drivers in Zephyr. Our first prototype did 500sps and 2M BLE PHY in less than 24KB on a BT832A!
MAX86141 Arduino Arduino driver for the MAX86141
ADS131M08 Arduino Arduino driver and breakout board CAD files for the ADS131M08, a 24 bit sigma delta converter. Tested ~350nV noise on my breakout board with an ESP32. Dmitry (FreeEEG32) designed a working low cost 128 channel EEG prototype with this chip, while we based our nRF design with it.
FreeEEG32 Crowdfunding I helped Dmitry Sukhoruchkin and Bernard Markus crowdfund the FreeEEG32, which they developed hardware and custom 3D printed headsets for over the past several years. We are dealing with part shortages but otherwise have the lowest cost clinical-grade EEG ever made! We've had contacts worldwide with interesting scientists and investors in the space. AGPLv3
More PCB work I love open source DIY! I've made several HEG prototypes, several ADC breakout boards, high frequency antennas with Eric Nichols, and my favorite WIP project: DIY Diffuse Optical Tomography.
